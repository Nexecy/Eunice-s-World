A Little Description of My Future Dream

Ever since I was a child, I’ve dreamed of becoming a doctor. It was a vision fueled by curiosity, a deep desire to help others, and a fascination with the human body. I imagined myself in a white coat, saving lives and making a difference. As I grew older, that dream never wavered. When it was time to decide on a path, I initially planned to take a pre-med course in Medical Technology (MedTech), which I believed would be the perfect foundation for medical school.

However, life had different plans for me. My mother, always concerned about my health, was worried about the challenges I would face living far from home. I have a tendency to get sick easily, and the thought of being away from family support made her anxious. Her concern was understandable, and as much as I wanted to pursue my dream, I also didn’t want to cause her any worry.

After careful thought and conversations with my family, I decided to take up Nursing instead. At first, it wasn’t my ideal choice. I feared that once I got into the rhythm of nursing, I might hesitate to pursue medicine later. Nursing felt like a detour from the clear path I had envisioned. But life, as I’ve learned, often surprises us in the most unexpected ways.

Now, as a third-year nursing student, I’ve come to appreciate the beauty and complexity of this profession. Nursing is not just about care—it’s about compassion, resilience, and adaptability. The course is challenging, both academically and emotionally, but it has shaped me in ways I never anticipated. It’s pushed me to study harder, think critically, and develop a deep empathy for patients.

Although I may not consider myself the smartest in the room, I’ve learned that hard work and determination can bridge the gap. Every class, every clinical experience, and every late night of studying brings me closer to the person I want to become. Nursing has given me skills and insights that will undoubtedly serve me well when I eventually pursue medical school.

Looking ahead, my goal remains the same: to become a doctor. I now understand that the journey might not always be a straight line, but each step—no matter how unexpected—has its purpose. Nursing has taught me patience, perseverance, and the importance of human connection, all of which will make me a better physician someday.

This journey is far from over, and I know there will be more challenges ahead. But I am ready. My dream of becoming a doctor continues to guide me, and I am confident that, with determination and support from those I love, I will achieve it. For now, I am embracing where I am, trusting that every experience is shaping me into the person I am meant to be.

Paris, My Dream Place!

Paris, often called the “City of Love,” is a place that captures the imagination of dreamers around the world. Known for its romantic ambiance, rich history, and iconic landmarks, the city is a symbol of art, culture, and elegance.

The Eiffel Tower stands tall as its most famous landmark, offering breathtaking views of the city. The Seine River winds through Paris, with charming bridges and riverbanks perfect for leisurely strolls. Paris is also home to world-renowned museums like the Louvre, housing masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa.

In Paris, every street corner seems to tell a story. The cafes are filled with the aroma of freshly brewed coffee, and the city’s vibrant energy is palpable. Whether it’s exploring the artistic haven of Montmartre or indulging in pastries at a local patisserie, Paris offers an experience that feels like stepping into a dream.
